Why It Matters

Most People Get Generator Sizing Wrong

The biggest mistake homeowners make is calculating only running watts and ignoring startup (surge) watts. When an electric motor starts ‚Äî an air conditioner, refrigerator, sump pump, or well pump ‚Äî it draws 3 to 6 times its running wattage for a fraction of a second.

A 5,000W generator cannot start a 5,000W A/C unit. A generator that can't handle the surge will stall, trip its breaker, or fail entirely ‚Äî right when you need it most.

Running watts (also called rated watts) is the continuous power an appliance needs while operating normally. Starting watts (surge watts) is the brief, higher power spike required to start motors ‚Äî compressors, pumps, fans ‚Äî from a dead stop. Most appliances with electric motors draw 3‚Äì6√ó their running wattage for a fraction of a second at startup. Home standby generators (whole-home generators) start automatically, run on natural gas or propane, and connect directly to your home's electrical panel via an automatic transfer switch.
